Land degradation

It is the loss of value of a piece of land dure
to soil erosion , urbanisation , human activities ,overgrazing,
river siltation or defforestation.Increased demand for agricultue
products also is a major contribution to land degradation as large
tracts of land are cleared to accomodate it.

Causes of Land degradation

Agriculture

When land is cleared for agriculture a lot
happens.The organisms that were in the natural environment
are lost. Land is left bare at some point , making erosion
by wind and rain possible.

Defforestation

The trees help bind soil together. When they are removed
the soil is loosened making soil erosion possible. This soil can
swept into rivers causing river siltation.

Overgrazing

This is wereby cattle is left to graze aimlessly
without close supervision making it graze all/allmost
the pastureland available.This has the effect of causing
soil erosion as lan is left bare.This also reduces the ability
of plants to grow or water to penetrate the soil.

Riverbank Cultivation

This has the effect of blocking up rivers or
water sources commonly known as river siltation.
Aquatic life can be affected by this and can kill
it. Some chemicals can even find their way into
the rivers.

Mining activities

Mining has the effect of first causing defforestation
in the act destroying natural ecosystems.Destroying of
organisms and blockage of any river sources in the area.

Effects of land degradation

Desertification

Desertification can be described as a climate condition
that has frequent droughts , low to zero rainfall , dry spells and
above all hot temperatures during the day and cold during nights.
The effects of this include loss of biodiversity ,land degradation and
soil erosion among other things.

Loss of arable land

Arable land is land that can be used to grow crops.
With the washing away of top soil i.e soil erosion the soil
left is not suitable for crop cultivation. Other activities
such as mining also cause loss of soil arability.

Destruction of natural ecosystems

With the rapid growing population , alot of land is
cleared to make room for human settlement or agricultural
development.Organisms or other life forms are also cleared or
in the process.

River siltation or blockage of waterways

River bank cultivation of plants makes soil and chemicals
flow into the waterways making the rivers be blocked.Also mining
of minerals close to waterways blocks them and adds harnmful
chemicals.Industries that dump solid wastes in the waterways also
face risk of contaminating and blocking waterways.

Risk of flooding

With the convection of floodplains or wetlands into agricutural
or residential areas , risk of flooding is met.The lands will not
be able to absorb water as before .

Mitigation against Land degradation

  • Inforcement of legislation against streambank cultivation.
  • Creation of a policy that makes it compulsory for a mine
    to rehabilitate the area it mined on after its operations have ceased.
  • No cultivation policy on wetlands or floodlains.
  • Creation of paddocks in a effort to control overgrazing.
  • Creation of Afforestation programmes

Pollution

Types of pollution

  1. Water pollution
  2. Air pollution
  3. Land pollution
  4. Noise pollution
  1. Water pollution

    2. Water pollution is when water sources are contaminated.
    Various aquatic organisms depend on these waters so if they got
    contaminated they might die involving us humans who also depend on
    on these waters.

    Causes

    • Industrial waste dumped by in water sources creates an environment that is not chemically conducive for some animals
    • Fertilizers , pesticides e.t.c used by farmers may find their way into water sources or rivers ending up killing fish or other animals that depend on these waters.
    • Bathing or using the river as a toilet or bathing in rivers leaves chemicals in the river and makes the water be diseased so that if someone done the river uses this water might get sick.
    • Oil leaks of tanks at sea causes the pollution fo the water.

    Mitigation

    • Regulation against dumping untreated chemicals into water sources
    • Water treatment plants should be built to help reduce the pollution already incurred.
    • Preventation of bathing in rivers e.t.c and using proper facilities.
  2. Air pollution

    3. The contamination of the atmosphere is called air pollution.
    All organisms respire using the air around them , so if it gets
    contaminated some drastic effects can be witnessed. Air needs
    to be clean and free from poisonous gases all the time.

    Causes

    • Exhaust gases from vehicles that are not fully combusted.
    • Some industries which produce gases like sulphur dioxide and carbon monoxide which mix the air and clouds to form acid rain.
    • Burning of fossil fuels and other plastics produces some harmful gases.

    Mitigation

    • Use of catalytic converters in vehicles .
    • Preventing the burning of used products
    • Development of a method to rid of gases produced by industries.
  3. Land pollution

    4. Each household produces tonnes of garbage each
    year. Garbage like aluminium, plastic, paper, cloth,
    wood is collected and sent to the local recycling unit.
    Items that can not be recycled become a part of the
    landfills that hampers the beauty of the city and cause
    land pollution.

    Causes

    • Solid waste from homes, restaraunts, public places, markets, offices, and so on. Because most of these waste materials are non-biodegradable they heap in the landfills where they stay for years.
    • Agricultural waste from animal manure, crop and farm remains. The solid wastes like the animal manure and other agricultural by products are collected and dumped in the landfills.
    • Industrial processes, construction activities and power plants produce a wide range of solid by-products and residues. The predominant waste products are generated from oil refineries, power plants, construction works, pharmaceuticalsnand so on.
    • Poor enforcement of legislation against littering.
    • Inadequate dumping places
    • Uncollection of wastes
    • Rapid population growth without adequate city planning.

    Mitigation

    • Design and implementation of intergrated waste management system.
    • Enforcement of existing legislation against waste management.
    • Collection and clean up of existing overcrowded landfills.
    • Improvement of the existing collection system of wastes.
    • Proactive city planning by city fathers to cater for the rapidly growing population.
