What is a sample population

A sample population is defined as the process of selecting a subset of a randomized number of members of the population of a study and collecting data about their attributes.
According to Sekeran(2000:38) , "Sampling is a process of selecting a sufficiant number of elements from the population so that by studying the sample and understanding the properties and characteristics of the population ."

The main objectives for sampling are that:

  • It is possible to collect data from or test or examine every element
  • The study can lead to more reliable results because fatigue is reduced resulting in fewer errors in collecting data.

e.g A sample can consist of 35 respondents of which are vendors when sudying the informal sector.

Tips on selecting a population sample

  • Appropriate population.
  • Appropriate sampling method.
