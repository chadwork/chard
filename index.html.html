Methods of collecting data

Questionnaire

Johnston (1997:151) defines a questionnaire as a list of structured questions which are marked or given to a selected sample of individuals who record their responses and send back the questionnaire.

According to wlonick (1993) , questionnaires are one of the most popular methods of conducting scholarly research. Questionnaires can either be structured or unstructured.Structured questions consist of a set of questions , which have blank spaces to be filled in by the respondents whereas non-structured questionnaires are open ended.

Tips on using questionnaire

  • State the population sample
  • State the location
  • State the age group
  • State gender if applicable
  • State the timeframe for the collection and distrubution of the questionnaire
  • Have a budget e.g for photocopying , printing e.t.c
Example of questionnaire

Advantages of using questionnaire

  • Reduces manipulated or corrupted response as the respondents give their personal opnions without others influence
  • Questionnaires are familiar to most respondents
  • Anonymity and privacy is incouraged by the questionnaire

Disadvantages

  • Production of questionnaires is expensive dure to ink , bondpaper , printing e.t.c
  • Misinterpretation of questions by respondents since clarity cannot be sort
  • They are not flexible as interviews. There is no possibility of probing further on the question

Interview

Holborn (2002:108), interviews are face to face meetings between the interviewer and the interviewee. According to Zikmund(2003) interviews typically ask oral questions of individuals .

Responses are recorded through taking down of notes , audio/visual equipment recordings and computers typing , during or following the interview .

Tips on using Interview

  • Should make an apponent.
  • Be specific , don`t be too long.
  • Have a written questionnaire.
  • Your questionnaire should include Yes or No responses.
  • Dress appropriately for the interview.
  • Know your audience.
  • Should be respectful.
Sample of interview questions

Avantages of using an interview

  • These is more flexibility
  • You can get information that is complex and emotional
  • The researcher can use non-verbal communication during interviews and read facial expressions or gestures on response of sensitive issues .
  • Interviews enable the researcher to control the response situation by scheduling time , place and the sequence of the questions.
  • the researcher can gain trust and support from the respondent.

Disadvantages

  • It includes the difficulty in standardazing the interview situation so that the interviewer does not influence the respondents answers.
  • Interviews cannot provide anomynity for the respondent.

Study skills

With this methods i believe you can pass your exam.


  1. Create a reading timetable.

    2. One of the main keys of being an effective and successful student is being able
    to organize your work.You need to create a timetable where by you cater for every subject
    to forgeting to give yourself breaks where you can rest or relax your brain.


  2. Read at the sametime everyday.

    3. It is very important to plan a constant, daily study routine.
    When you study at the sametime every day or week it becomes adapted to your body.
    You will become mentally and emotionally more prepared for each study session
    making you more productive and efficient.


  3. Should set yourself a specific goal to archieve for every study session.

    4. Just studying for the sake of it leads you nowhere.You need to set
    for yourself clear goals that you can achieve per study session.
    e.g know all the types of families in heritage studies.


  4. DO NOT try to cram all topics or subject areas in one study session.

    5. Do not try to do all the stuff at the same time , you not a machine.
    You should not keep up long hours while studying , trust me that most
    of the information will be gone when you fall asleep.
    As stated
    you should have a timetable to this.


  5. Have a conducive study area of your own.

    6. Study somewhere you know you will not be distracted or disturbed.
    Somewhere there is no too much noise pollution. You can even change
    environments per study session to help the brain be able to differentiate
    the information gained at different intervals. You study area should be
    as comfortable as possible.


  6. Always review your notes before starting a task/assignment.

    7. Almost all Zimbabwean government schools are given notes in class

    given task or assignment.This will help you remember important
    topical points.

    can also help each other complete tasks or assignments more
